Cecil King (1921-1986): Present in Time Future

Cecil King had a long and rich association with the Hugh Lane Gallery. His first retrospective was held here in 1981. It is therefore with great pleasure that we present King’s work from the collection to mark the 100th anniversary of his birth.

Ancient Ground by Willie Doherty

Much of Doherty's work has been shaped by his experiences growing up in a society deeply divided by political and religious conflict His work explores the distortions that inevitably occur in the representation of reality.

Stained Glass Room

Harry Clarke piece The Eve of St. Agnes and the recently acquired 'Mr Gilhooley by Liam O'Flaherty' for the Geneva Window The room also features work by Wilhelmina Geddes, Evie Hone, Paul Bony and James Scanlon.

Sean Scully Room

As a result of an ongoing collaboration with Sean Scully,
the gallery is honoured to be the recipient of a gift of eight paintings presented by the artist in 2006 and Landline Gray, donated in 2018.

The Lane Legacy

The Lane Legacy exhibition, which was launched by Michael D Higgins on 20 January 2020, celebrates the gallery’s founder, the philanthropist Sir Hugh Lane.
